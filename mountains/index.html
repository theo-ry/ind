Fold Mountains

These mountains form when two plates collide head on and their edges crumple, much like how a piece of paper folds when pushed together.

Block Mountains

These mountains form when faults or cracks in the earth's crust force some materials or blocks of rock up and others down.

Dome Mountains

These mountains form when a great amount of magma pushes the rock layers above it up without erupting onto the surface then cools into hardened rock.

Volcanic Mountains

These mountains are formed when magma erupts and piles upon the surface creating many layers that when cooled, builds a cone of rock.

Plateau Mountains

These mountains are formed by erosion. As years pass, streams and rivers erode valleys through the plateau, leaving mountains standing between the valleys.

Fact 1

Mountains are known to be able to slow down storms moving from the ocean, causing rain to drop.

Fact 2

One side of a mountain is usually known to be drier than the other side.

Fact 3

Mountains make up about one-fifth of the world's landscape, and provide homes to at least one-tenth of the world's people.

Fact 4

The tallest known mountain in the solar system is Olympus Mons, which is located on Mars.

Fact 5

Mountains occur more often in oceans than on land; some islands are the peaks of mountains coming out of the water.

Fact 6

About 80 per cent of our planet's fresh water is known to originate in the mountains.
